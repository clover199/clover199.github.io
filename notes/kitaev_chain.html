Hamiltonians equivalent under transformations
Complex fermions (1D p-wave superconductor) $$ H = \sum_j \left[ -t ( c^{\dagger}_{j} c_{j+1} - c_{j} c_{j+1}^{\dagger} ) + \gamma ( c_{j} c_{j+1} - c_{j}^{\dagger} c_{j+1}^{\dagger} ) + 2h c^{\dagger}_{j} c_{j} \right] $$ Define \(\Psi^{\dagger}_j=(c^{\dagger}_j, c_j)\), we can rewrite the Hamiltonian as $$ H = \sum_j \left[ \Psi^{\dagger}_{j} \left( - \frac{t}{2} \tau_z - \frac{\gamma}{2} i\tau_y \right) \Psi_{j+1} + h.c. \right] + h \sum_j \Psi^{\dagger}_{j} \tau_z \Psi_{j}$$ Majorana fermions $$ H_{\mathrm{M}} = \sum_j \left[ \frac{-t+\gamma}{2}\; ia_j b_{j+1} + \frac{t+\gamma}{2}\; ib_ja_{j+1} + h \;ia_jb_j \right] $$ Spin-\(\frac{1}{2}\) $$ H_{\mathrm{XY}} = \sum_{j} \left[ \frac{t+\gamma}{2} \sigma_{j}^x \sigma_{j+1}^x + \frac{t-\gamma}{2} \sigma_{j}^y \sigma_{j+1}^y + h \sigma_{j}^z \right] $$
Transformations
Majorana fermion $$ a_j = c_j + c_j^{\dagger}, \;\; i b_j = c_j - c_j^{\dagger} $$ some relations $$ ia_jb_j = 2c_j^{\dagger}c_j-1 $$ Jordan-Wigner transformation $$ c_j = \left( \prod_{k=1}^{j-1} \sigma_k^z \right) \frac{\sigma_j^x - i \sigma_j^y}{2} $$ some relations $$ c_j^{\dagger} c_j = \frac{1+\sigma_j^z}{2} $$ $$ c_j^{\dagger} c_{j+1} + h.c. = -\frac{1}{2} ( \sigma_{j}^x \sigma_{j+1}^x + \sigma_{j}^y \sigma_{j+1}^y ) $$ $$ c_j c_{j+1} + h.c. = \frac{1}{2} ( \sigma_{j}^x \sigma_{j+1}^x - \sigma_{j}^y \sigma_{j+1}^y ) $$
Bloch structure
For a chain of length \(L\) with periodic boundary condition $$ c^{\dagger}_j = \sum_k e^{-ikj} c^{\dagger}_k, \;\; k=\frac{2\pi n}{L}$$ Bogoliubov-de-Gennes (BdG) formalism
$$ H = \Psi^{\dagger}_k \left( \begin{array}{cc} -t\cos k + h & - i \gamma \sin k \\ i \gamma \sin k & t\cos k - h \end{array} \right) \Psi_k $$ where \(\Psi^{\dagger}_k=(c^{\dagger}_k,\;c_{-k})\). $$\mathcal{H}(k)=\tau_y \gamma\sin k + \tau_z (h-t\cos k) $$ Energy spectrum $$E_{\pm} = \pm \sqrt{(t\cos k - h)^2+(\gamma \sin k)^2} $$ Symmetries $$\mathcal{T}=\mathcal{K}, \;\; \mathcal{P}=\tau_x\mathcal{K}, \;\; \mathcal{C}=\tau_x$$
