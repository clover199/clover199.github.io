Drude model wiki
Goal: get finite conductivity by impurity scattering
\(q\): electron charge
\(n\): charge density
\(\tau\): average time between subsequent collisions
\(\mathbf{E}\): uniform and constant electric field
\(\mathbf{J}\): current density
Static
$$\mathbf{J} = \left( \frac{nq^2\tau}{m} \right) \mathbf{E} \equiv \sigma_0 \mathbf{E}$$ derived from $$\langle\mathbf{p}\rangle = q\mathbf{E}\tau = m\langle \mathbf{v}\rangle,\quad \mathbf{J} = nq\langle \mathbf{v}\rangle$$ Dynamic
$$\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}t}\langle\mathbf{p}(t)\rangle = q\left(\mathbf{E}+\frac{\langle\mathbf{p}(t)\rangle\times\mathbf{B}}{m}\right)- \frac{\langle\mathbf{p}(t)\rangle}{\tau}$$ First terms is external force. Second term is from effects of collisions.
When \(\mathbf{E}=\mathrm{Re}()\mathbf{E_0e^{iwt}})\) $$\sigma(w) = \frac{\sigma_0}{1+iw\tau}$$ Accuracy of model
Accurate when \(\tau\) is large. Otherwise band structure plays a more important role.
Replace electron mass \(m\) by effect mass from band theory \(m^*\)
